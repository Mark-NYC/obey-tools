The Command
Love God with Everything

When a scribe asked Jesus which commandment was greatest, Jesus gave a two-part answer that summarizes the entire law. First: love God with every part of your being. This is the foundation of worship — not a style of music or a Sunday service, but a total orientation of heart, soul, mind, and strength toward God.

"The most important one," answered Jesus, "is this: 'Hear, O Israel: The Lord our God, the Lord is one. Love the Lord your God with all your heart and with all your soul and with all your mind and with all your strength.'"

— Mark 12:29–30

True worship is not confined to a building or a time slot. It is a posture of the whole person directed toward God in every moment of every day. "Heart, soul, mind, and strength" — nothing is withheld. No part of you lives outside the reach of worship. Paul would later say to "offer your bodies as a living sacrifice" — worship is how you live, not just something you do on Sundays.

The Story
The Sinful Woman — Luke 7:36–50

Jesus was invited to dinner at the home of Simon, a Pharisee. In the middle of the meal, a woman who was known in town as a sinner came in. She stood behind Jesus at his feet, weeping. Her tears fell on his feet, and she wiped them with her hair, kissed them, and poured expensive perfume on them.

Simon was disgusted. He thought to himself: if this man were truly a prophet, he would know what kind of woman was touching him. Jesus, reading his thoughts, told him a story about two debtors — one who owed a huge sum and one who owed very little. Both were forgiven. Which one would love more?

Simon replied, "I suppose the one who had the bigger debt forgiven." "You have judged correctly," Jesus said.

— Luke 7:43

Then Jesus turned to the woman and spoke directly about her extravagant worship:

"Therefore, I tell you, her many sins have been forgiven — as her great love has shown. But whoever has been forgiven little loves little."

— Luke 7:47

Simon had given Jesus no water for his feet, no greeting kiss, no oil for his head. He had checked the religious boxes but had no love. This woman, who understood how much she had been forgiven, gave everything she had. Worship is the overflow of a heart that understands grace.

Reflect
Questions for Discussion
  • Jesus says the woman loved much because she was forgiven much. How deeply do you feel the weight of what you've been forgiven? How does that affect how you worship?
  • Simon went through the motions without love. In what ways might you be "Simonlike" — religious on the outside but reserved in your heart toward God?
  • What does it mean to worship God with your "mind" and "strength"? Can you give a concrete example from your own life?
  • The woman's worship was costly, public, and uninhibited. What holds you back from worshiping God freely?
Obey
Take a Step

Set aside 15 minutes for undistracted worship this week — no phone, no agenda. Put on music that helps you focus on God, or sit in silence. Tell him specifically what you love about him and what you're grateful for.

Ask yourself: what is the most costly thing you could give God right now? Not money — your time, your pride, your fear, your comfort. Bring it to him as an act of worship, like the woman's perfume.
