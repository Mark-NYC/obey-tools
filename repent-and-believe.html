Repent & Believe
Mk 1:15 · Command 1 of 12
The Command
Turn and Trust

Jesus began his public ministry with a single, urgent call: repent and believe. To repent means to turn — to change your mind and direction. To believe means to trust completely, staking your life on Jesus and the good news he brings.

"The time has come. The kingdom of God has come near. Repent and believe the good news!"

— Mark 1:15

This is not a one-time transaction but a lifelong posture — continually turning toward God and continually trusting in Jesus. It is the entry point and the ongoing rhythm of the Christian life.

The Story
Zacchaeus — Luke 19:1–10

Zacchaeus was a chief tax collector in Jericho — wealthy, powerful, and despised. He cheated people and collaborated with the Roman occupiers. When Jesus passed through town, Zacchaeus was so desperate to see him that he climbed a sycamore-fig tree.

When Jesus reached the spot, he looked up and said to him, "Zacchaeus, come down immediately. I must stay at your house today." So he came down at once and welcomed him gladly.

— Luke 19:5–6

The crowd grumbled — Jesus was going to eat with a sinner. But something happened at that meal. Zacchaeus stood up and declared he would give half his possessions to the poor and repay anyone he had cheated four times over.

Jesus said to him, "Today salvation has come to this house… For the Son of Man came to seek and to save the lost."

— Luke 19:9–10

Zacchaeus did not clean himself up before coming to Jesus — Jesus came to him first. The encounter transformed him. Real repentance produces real change: his money, his reputation, his relationships — all of it reoriented toward God and others.

Reflect
Questions for Discussion
  • What area of your life have you been holding back from God — something you haven't fully turned over?
  • Zacchaeus made specific, costly restitution. What would genuine repentance look like in your situation?
  • Jesus said he "must" stay at Zacchaeus's house. What does it say about Jesus that he pursued someone so hated by the community?
  • How is believing the good news different from just agreeing it is true intellectually?
Obey
Take a Step

This week, identify one specific area where you need to repent — where you have been going your own way. Write it down. Then bring it to Jesus in prayer, confess it to a trusted friend, and take one concrete step to make it right.

Practice believing the good news by memorizing Mark 1:15 and repeating it each morning this week as a declaration over your day.
