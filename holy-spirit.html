Holy Spirit
Acts 4:27–31 · Command 3 of 12
The Command
Receive the Spirit

Jesus promised his followers that they would receive power when the Holy Spirit came upon them. He commanded them to wait for this gift before going out to be his witnesses. The Spirit is not an optional upgrade — he is the source of all kingdom power, comfort, and guidance in the believer's life.

"And I will ask the Father, and he will give you another advocate to help you and be with you forever — the Spirit of truth."

— John 14:16–17

Jesus told his disciples to wait in Jerusalem until they received the Holy Spirit — and he told them it was better that he go away so the Spirit could come. The Spirit convicts the world of sin, guides believers into truth, and empowers the church for mission. To live without the Spirit is to try to obey in your own strength — which will always fall short.

"But you will receive power when the Holy Spirit comes on you; and you will be my witnesses in Jerusalem, and in all Judea and Samaria, and to the ends of the earth."

— Acts 1:8

The Story
The Empowered Church — Acts 4:27–31

The early church had just been threatened by the same religious leaders who had killed Jesus. Peter and John had been arrested, interrogated, and ordered to stop speaking about Jesus. When they returned to the community and reported what had happened, the whole group lifted their voices in prayer together.

"Now, Lord, consider their threats and enable your servants to speak your word with great boldness. Stretch out your hand to heal and perform signs and wonders through the name of your holy servant Jesus."

— Acts 4:29–30

They did not pray for safety or for the threats to go away. They prayed for boldness. And God answered:

After they prayed, the place where they were meeting was shaken. And they were all filled with the Holy Spirit and spoke the word of God boldly.

— Acts 4:31

Notice the sequence: they prayed together, the Spirit filled them, and they immediately went out and spoke boldly. The Holy Spirit does not fill people so they can have a private spiritual experience — he fills people for witness and mission. The Spirit empowers us to do what we cannot do in our own courage.

Reflect
Questions for Discussion
  • When have you experienced the Holy Spirit giving you words or courage you didn't have on your own?
  • The disciples prayed for boldness, not safety. What does that tell us about how to pray under pressure?
  • Are you more aware of the Spirit's presence in your daily life, or do you tend to forget about him? What might change if you relied on him more intentionally?
  • What would it look like in your context for the Holy Spirit to "shake the place" where you meet?
Obey
Take a Step

Each morning this week, begin your day by inviting the Holy Spirit to fill you, guide you, and speak through you. Don't start the day in your own strength — ask him to lead before you take a step.

Pray the same prayer as the early church: "Lord, give me boldness." Specifically ask for one opportunity this week to share your faith — and watch how the Spirit shows up when you step out.
