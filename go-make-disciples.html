Go…Make Disciples
Matt 28:18–20 · Command 11 of 12
The Command
The Great Commission

The last words of Jesus before his ascension are his marching orders for the church of every generation. Every believer is sent. This is not a calling reserved for missionaries or pastors — it is the commission of every disciple. The command is not to bring people to church; it is to make disciples wherever you go.

"All authority in heaven and on earth has been given to me. Therefore go and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit, and teaching them to obey everything I have commanded you. And surely I am with you always, to the very end of the age."

— Matthew 28:18–20

The main verb is "make disciples" — everything else serves that. Going, baptizing, and teaching are how you make disciples. Notice what Jesus does not say: "make converts" or "get decisions." A disciple is someone who is learning to obey everything Jesus commanded. That takes relationship, time, and intentionality. Jesus modeled exactly this with his twelve — and he sends us to do the same.

The Story
The Seventy-Two — Luke 10:1–11

Before the Great Commission, Jesus had already been practicing it. He appointed seventy-two disciples and sent them out two by two into every town and place he was about to go — like advance teams preparing the way. He gave them specific instructions:

"The harvest is plentiful, but the workers are few. Ask the Lord of the harvest, therefore, to send out workers into his harvest field. Go! I am sending you out like lambs among wolves."

— Luke 10:2–3

He told them to stay in one household, eat what was provided, heal the sick, and announce: "The kingdom of God has come near to you." The mission was relational, local, and vulnerable — lambs among wolves, not soldiers with weapons. When they returned, they were overjoyed:

The seventy-two returned with joy and said, "Lord, even the demons submit to us in your name." He replied, "I saw Satan fall like lightning from heaven."

— Luke 10:17–18

Jesus sent ordinary people, not trained professionals. He sent them in pairs, with no money bag or extra sandals, into unfamiliar places. And through their simple presence and proclamation, the kingdom broke through. The model is the same today: go, stay, eat, heal, proclaim.

Reflect
Questions for Discussion
  • Do you see yourself as "sent"? Or do you think of mission as someone else's job? What shaped that belief?
  • Jesus sent the 72 in pairs. Who is your partner in mission? If you don't have one, who could you invite into that role?
  • The 72 were told to stay in one household and build relationship. Who in your neighborhood, workplace, or school is your "household of peace" — someone open to you?
  • Making disciples means "teaching them to obey everything I have commanded." Who are you currently investing in to help them grow as a follower of Jesus?
Obey
Take a Step

Write down three names of people in your life who don't yet follow Jesus. Commit to praying for them every day this week, looking for natural opportunities to share your story and invite them further.

Think of a younger or newer believer in your life. Invite them to meet regularly — even once a month — to read scripture together, share life, and encourage each other to obey what Jesus commanded. That is making disciples.
