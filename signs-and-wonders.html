Signs & Wonders
Jn 14:12–14 · Command 8 of 12
The Command
Greater Things

One of the most audacious statements Jesus ever made came on the night before his death. He told his disciples that anyone who believes in him would do "even greater things" than he had done. This is not a metaphor. Jesus healed the sick, raised the dead, and cast out demons. And he said his followers would do more.

"Very truly I tell you, whoever believes in me will do the works I have been doing, and they will do even greater things than these, because I am going to the Father. And I will do whatever you ask in my name, so that the Father may be glorified in the Son."

— John 14:12–13

The "greater things" are possible because Jesus would send the Holy Spirit to empower his followers across the whole world. Jesus's earthly ministry was geographically limited. But through his Spirit-filled church, his works would multiply to the ends of the earth. Signs and wonders are meant to accompany the proclamation of the gospel — pointing people to Jesus, the source of all healing and freedom.

The Story
Through the Roof — Luke 5:17–26

Jesus was teaching in a house so packed that no one could get in or out. Outside, four men arrived carrying their paralyzed friend on a mat — desperate, determined, and blocked at every turn. So they climbed up on the roof and dug through it, lowering their friend through the hole into the room, right in front of Jesus.

When Jesus saw their faith, he said, "Friend, your sins are forgiven."

— Luke 5:20

The religious teachers in the room were outraged — only God could forgive sins. Jesus knew their thoughts and responded by connecting the visible miracle to the invisible one:

"But I want you to know that the Son of Man has authority on earth to forgive sins." So he said to the paralyzed man, "I tell you, get up, take your mat and go home." Immediately he stood up in front of them, took what he had been lying on and went home praising God.

— Luke 5:24–25

The physical healing was proof of something deeper: Jesus has authority to deal with what is broken at the root. The friends' faith — their willingness to tear the roof off to bring someone to Jesus — was the instrument God used. Signs and wonders follow faith that refuses to give up.

Reflect
Questions for Discussion
  • The four friends went to extreme lengths to get their paralyzed friend to Jesus. Who in your life needs to be "carried" to Jesus — and are you willing to be that kind of friend?
  • Jesus saw "their faith" (plural) before healing the man. What does it say about intercession — praying for others — that the friends' faith played a role?
  • Do you believe Jesus still heals today? If you're uncertain, what has shaped that belief (or unbelief)?
  • Jesus promised "even greater things." Does that promise feel real to you, or distant? What would it take for you to begin praying with that expectation?
Obey
Take a Step

Think of someone you know who is sick, oppressed, or in desperate need. Pray for them specifically and boldly this week — not a vague "God bless them" but a specific request for God to heal or intervene. Then tell them you prayed.

The next time you sense someone is hurting, ask if you can pray for them right then and there — not later, not "I'll pray for you," but now. Step out in faith and watch what God does.
