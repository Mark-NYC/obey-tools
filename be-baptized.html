Be Baptized
Matt. 28:19 · Command 2 of 12
The Command
Go Under the Water

Baptism is not optional — it is one of the commands of Christ. Jesus himself was baptized, and he commanded his followers to baptize new disciples as part of making them. Baptism is the public declaration that you belong to Jesus and have died to your old life.

"Therefore go and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit."

— Matthew 28:19

Baptism is a burial and a resurrection. Going under the water pictures dying with Christ. Coming up out of the water pictures rising to new life. It is your outward testimony to an inward transformation — the moment you publicly cross the line and identify as a follower of Jesus.

The Story
Philip & The Ethiopian — Acts 8:26–39

Philip was prompted by an angel to travel a desert road. There he met an Ethiopian official — a high-ranking treasurer of Queen Candace — sitting in his chariot, reading aloud from the scroll of Isaiah. Philip ran up and heard him reading:

"He was led like a sheep to the slaughter, and as a lamb before its shearer is silent, so he did not open his mouth."

— Acts 8:32 (quoting Isaiah 53:7)

The official asked who Isaiah was talking about. Philip explained it was Jesus — and starting from that very passage, he told him the good news about Jesus. As they traveled, they came to some water:

The eunuch said, "Look, here is water. What can stand in the way of my being baptized?" And he gave orders to stop the chariot. Then both Philip and the eunuch went down into the water and Philip baptized him.

— Acts 8:36–38

There was no delay, no lengthy membership class, no waiting for the perfect conditions. When he believed, he was baptized. And when he came up out of the water, he went on his way rejoicing. Baptism followed faith immediately — and joy followed baptism.

Reflect
Questions for Discussion
  • Have you been baptized as a believer? If not, what has kept you from taking that step?
  • The Ethiopian asked "What can stand in the way?" — is anything standing in your way right now?
  • Why do you think Jesus commanded baptism rather than leaving it optional? What does it accomplish that a private decision doesn't?
  • The official was baptized immediately. What does that urgency tell us about how seriously early believers took this command?
Obey
Take a Step

If you have not been baptized as a believer, make a plan to be baptized this month. Talk to a pastor or a mature Christian who can walk you through it. Don't wait for the perfect moment — the right time is when you believe.

If you are already baptized, share your baptism story with someone this week who hasn't heard it. Your testimony of going public with your faith could be the thing that moves someone else to take the same step.
