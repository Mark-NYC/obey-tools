The Word of God
Jn 8:31–32 · Command 4 of 12
The Command
Hold to His Teaching

Jesus says that freedom comes not from hearing the Word but from holding to it — remaining in it, abiding in it. The Word of God is not meant to be studied once and filed away. It is alive and active, meant to be lived in daily, like a branch living in a vine.

"If you hold to my teaching, you are really my disciples. Then you will know the truth, and the truth will set you free."

— John 8:31–32

Notice what Jesus promises: not just knowledge, but freedom. Many people know the Bible intellectually but haven't experienced the freedom it promises — because freedom comes from holding to it, not just knowing it. The Word read and obeyed produces transformation. The Word read and ignored produces a hardened heart.

The Story
I Am the Vine — John 15:1–17

On the night before his crucifixion, Jesus gave his disciples one of the most vivid pictures of spiritual life in all of Scripture. He described himself as a vine and his followers as branches. The central word he used over and over was "remain" — or "abide."

"I am the vine; you are the branches. If you remain in me and I in you, you will bear much fruit; apart from me you can do nothing."

— John 15:5

A branch that is cut from the vine doesn't slowly decline — it immediately begins to die. Fruit is not produced by trying harder; it is the natural result of staying connected to the vine. Jesus then connects this directly to his Word:

"If you remain in me and my words remain in you, ask whatever you wish, and it will be done for you."

— John 15:7

His words remaining in you is the condition for answered prayer, for fruitfulness, for joy. The Word of God is not one discipline among many — it is the lifeline. When we neglect it, we cut ourselves off from the very source of everything we need.

Reflect
Questions for Discussion
  • How consistent is your current practice of reading and meditating on God's Word? What gets in the way?
  • Jesus says "apart from me you can do nothing." Where in your life have you been trying to bear fruit without staying connected to the vine?
  • What is the difference between reading the Bible to check a box and truly letting his words "remain in you"?
  • Can you think of a time when a specific scripture set you free from something — fear, shame, confusion, or a lie you believed?
Obey
Take a Step

Set a specific daily time to read the Word this week — morning, lunch, or night. Don't aim for a long session; aim for consistency. Even 10 minutes of focused, prayerful reading beats an hour of distracted skimming.

Pick one verse from this week's reading and memorize it. Write it on a card, put it somewhere visible, and say it aloud throughout the day. Let his words stay with you, not just pass through you.
